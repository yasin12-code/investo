EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================

The Lahore property market demonstrated robust performance in 2025,
with average property prices increasing by approximately 18% year-
over-year across all major housing societies and commercial zones.

This growth was driven by strong demand from overseas Pakistanis,
infrastructure development including the Orange Line Metro extension,
and favorable demographic trends in the 25-40 age bracket.

================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================

1. PRICE PERFORMANCE
   - Average price per marla in DHA Phase 6: PKR 18,000,000
   - Year-over-year growth: 18.2% (based on registered transactions)
   - Bahria Town Lahore growth: 16.7%
   - Gulberg and Model Town: 10-12% growth
   - Johar Town: 11.4% growth

2. TRANSACTION VOLUME
   - Total registered property transactions in Lahore: 45,230
   - Increase over 2024: 8.5%
   - Average transaction value: PKR 25,000,000

3. RENTAL MARKET
   - Average rental yield across Lahore: 2.5%
   - DHA Phase 6 rental yield: 1.9-2.1%
   - Bahria Town rental yield: 2.3-2.5%
   - Johar Town rental yield: 3.0-3.5% (highest yield area)

4. SUPPLY AND DEMAND
   - New housing units delivered in 2025: 12,500
   - Estimated housing demand: 18,000 units per year
   - Supply deficit: approximately 5,500 units
   - Vacancy rates remain low at 7-8% in premium areas

================================================================
MARKET OUTLOOK 2026
================================================================

We project continued strong growth in Lahore property prices:
- Expected price appreciation: 15-18% in 2026
- DHA and Bahria Town to lead growth
- Commercial property expected to outperform residential
- Rental yields expected to stabilize at current levels

KEY GROWTH DRIVERS:
- Continued infrastructure investment (Ring Road, Metro extensions)
- Overseas Pakistani remittances (projected $35B nationally)
- Population growth and urbanization (Lahore growing at 3.5% annually)
- Government housing incentives under Naya Pakistan Housing scheme
- Expected SBP rate cuts making mortgages more affordable

================================================================
RISK FACTORS
================================================================

- Political instability could deter overseas investment
- Inflation remains elevated at 12%
- Documentation requirements may slow cash transactions
- Global economic slowdown could reduce remittances
- Potential oversupply in apartment segment in DHA Phase 8-9

================================================================
INVESTMENT RECOMMENDATION
================================================================

Based on our analysis, Lahore property remains an attractive
investment opportunity with expected returns of 15-18% (capital
appreciation) plus 2-3% (rental yield) for a total return
potential of 17-21% annually.

We rate Lahore property as BUY for investors with:
- Budget: PKR 20,000,000+
- Time horizon: 3-5 years
- Risk tolerance: Moderate to High
- Liquidity needs: Low (property is illiquid)

================================================================
METHODOLOGY NOTE
================================================================

This report is based on:
- 12,450 registered property transactions in Q3-Q4 2025
- Price data from Punjab Land Revenue Authority (PLRA)
- Survey of 200 licensed property dealers
- Macroeconomic data from State Bank of Pakistan

Note: Prices used are registered prices which may differ from
actual transaction prices. Registered prices tend to be higher
than market transaction prices due to documentation requirements.
