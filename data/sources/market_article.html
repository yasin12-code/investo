Lahore Property Market Shows Signs of Cooling After Years of Rapid Growth

By Aisha Malik | January 10, 2026 | Pakistan Real Estate Weekly

Market Analysis

After several years of double-digit growth, Lahore's property market is showing clear signs of cooling. Our analysis of recent transaction data suggests that year-over-year price growth has slowed to approximately 8%, a significant decline from the 15-20% gains seen in 2022-2024.

Key Findings

  • Average property prices in DHA Phase 6 grew only 8.2% YoY in Q4 2025, down from 19.2% in Q4 2024
  • Transaction volumes have declined by 12% compared to the same period last year
  • Rental yields continue to compress, now averaging just 2.0% across premium locations
  • New housing supply has increased by 25% as developers complete delayed projects
  • Bahria Town Lahore shows slightly better performance at 10.5% growth but still below historical averages

Market Drivers

Several factors are contributing to the slowdown:

  • Interest rate environment: Despite SBP cuts, borrowing costs remain elevated at 17.5%
  • Oversupply: New apartment complexes in DHA and Bahria have increased available inventory by 30%
  • Regulatory changes: New tax documentation requirements have discouraged cash-based transactions
  • Alternative investments: Stock market rally and high bond yields are diverting capital from property

Price Predictions

We expect Lahore property prices to grow at 6-8% annually over the next 2-3 years, which is below inflation and represents a negative real return for many investors. Investors seeking higher returns should consider diversifying into REITs, mutual funds, or government bonds, which currently offer more attractive risk-adjusted returns.

Contradicting Reports

Note: The Punjab Revenue Authority's recent report claimed property prices grew by 18% in 2025. However, our independent analysis based on actual transaction data from 500+ verified sales shows growth closer to 8%. The discrepancy may be due to the PRA's reliance on listed prices rather than actual transaction prices, and their sample being skewed toward high-end properties in newer developments.
